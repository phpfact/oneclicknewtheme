Eligibility Criteria

Here are key Eligibility parameters to apply Canada PR

Age Factor

You must be at least 18 years old to apply for Canada PR. Age plays an important role in the Canada PR process because the sooner you apply, the better your chances. Up to 12 points will be allotted to candidates between the ages of 18 and 35 years. This is the maximum score. Each year above the age of 35, the points allocation will reduce by 1, with no points being awarded as of age 47.

Language Proficiency Test

Only those candidates who are capable of demonstrating an intermediate to high-level proficiency in one of Canada’s official languages, English or French, will be considered. You must prove your proficiency (minimum CLB 7) in one of these languages. Higher language proficiency can lead to an allocation of up to 24 points. You need to showcase language proficiency test results from an approved language testing agency, such as IELTS for English proficiency.

Educational Credential Assessment

The maximum number of points awarded for education is 25, with maximum points awarded with doctoral degrees. Foreign credentials will be evaluated by a designated third party also known as Educational Credential Assessment and points will be awarded based on that equivalence. At this time, organizations designated for credential evaluation are:

  • World Education Services
  • Medical Council of Canada
  • International Credential Assessment Service of Canada
  • Comparative Education Service: University of Toronto School of Continuing Studies

Work Experience

The more you have work experience, the higher your chances of getting a good score. Though, for applying Canada PR, a minimum of 1 year of work experience is required. To get an invitation to apply for Canada PR from IRCC, you must earn a high score on the point-based system of Canada. Also, work experience of at least 3 years can help you earn brownie points.

How to apply Canada PR

India is Canada’s most important source country for new immigrants. Applying for Canada PR is a systematic step-by-step procedure. Here are the six easy steps that you can follow for applying Canada PR:

6 EASY STEPS TO APPLY FOR CANADA PR

Step 1: Get your Educational Credential Done

As a foreign national applying for Canadian Permanent Residency, it is important to get your qualification verified, obtain Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) report from an approved assessing body, i.e. WES, ICASC, CES, ICES, etc. To move forward, it is essential to have a positive educational assessment done as it is the first step of applying for Canada PR.

Step 2:Score Minimum express entry points

As you are aware of the fact that Canada follows a point-based immigration system and to apply for Canada PR, you’re required to score minimum eligibility points based on your age, education, work experience, language proficiency, etc. The minimum score requirement is 67 points in the Express Entry skilled worker program. You can check your Canada PR score here!

Step 3:Create Express Entry Profile

As everything is systemised in the Canada PR process so is the application process! You are required to create an online profile in the express entry mentioning all the key details of your profile including work experience, ECA, IELTS score, and others. The candidates are ranked according to their profile, also known as the comprehensive ranking score which is awarded out of 1200 points.

Step 4:Wait for Canada’s Express Entry draw

IRCC will open an express entry draw to send the invitation to apply periodically. The candidates with the highest CRS score will get an invite to apply for Canada PR. Every month the Canadian Government invites highly ranked applicants for permanent residence. In fact, at the time of covid-19, the country issued more than 4700 invites. To know the CRS draw 2020 score, click here!



Step 5:Submit the Canada PR application

Once, you get the Invitation to Apply (ITA), submit a complete Canada PR application along with the required documents within 60 days of receiving the invite. Make sure you don’t commit mistakes or carelessness while submitting your application. A single mistake in terms of documentation or any other information can result in the cancellation of your invitation.



Step 6:Get Canada PR approval

This stage is the more impatient one because you don’t know what will be the final outcome of your application. While you are stressing out, the Canadian immigration authorities will check and verify your submitted application and documents. If everything is found valid, you will get approval for a Canadian PR visa.
